The Star Wars Guide to the 3AM Call

Remember the scene when Luke crashes on Dagobah, and discovers Yoda to begin his Jedi training? Those early scenes introduced Yoda’s pivotal character, providing the most memorable scenes in the series. Luke’s a rather young and raw recruit; as the pair walk around training, they find themselves near a cave, which Luke feels strange about. Yoda, of course, knows what it is.

That place… is strong with the dark side of the Force. A domain of evil it is. In you must go.

What’s in there?

Only what you take with you.

Yoda knows reality; Luke’s physical weapons won’t help him. What Luke counted on: blasters, weapons, and physical ability. What he needed: learn the way the world really works.

Luke had to learn the basic rule: you’ve got what you’ve got. Whether you’re prepared or not, ready or not, when serious social occasions pop up, you must handle them as you are — the time for preparation is over.

As an untrained rookie, Luke bumbles through the test, and in his haste, abandoned his training — a foolish decision causing problems later.

Worse, what he thought prepared him (blasters) served no purpose. Reality is … well … real. Too many people replace what works with worthless trinkets, replacing real tools with snake oil. Social justice, scholarship, modernizing the Bible … the dark side are those. Remember, truth is truth whether it’s popular or not, or believed or not.

Luke: I don’t believe it.

Yoda: That is why you fail.

To make it through, you must cast aside what doesn’t help, and pick up what does. Sadly, many people have those two lists backwards, because they fail to understand (as Yoda did) what really works, and what really equips you to respond.

What must we learn?

  • When entering the cave, you’ve got what you’ve got.
  • Preparation ends when trials come. You’re either ready or not.
  • Even a little training provides advantages.
  • Training never ends. You can always learn more.

Want to be prepared, do you? Begin now, you must. A plan to begin, is critical.

You will never find a more wretched hive of scum and villainy. We must be cautious.

Are you ready? If you have no idea where to begin, try our simple two-step program.

  1. Sign up for our newsletter, so you won’t miss anything.
  2. TELL US what you want to explore, and together we’ll help others.

Yoda restated our principle — at 3AM you’ve got what you’ve got.

… and it’s 2:59AM.

Stay in the Game

A terrible disease infects many Christians by believing life is easy and God grants everyone (with enough faith, that is) a Lexus, ice cream, and a pony. When life gets tough people hide the pain and frustration created by the paradox of what they think the Christian life should be—versus the reality they experience—by transforming into Tony the Tiger, where everything is grrrrreat! Yet everyone needs help sometimes.

Get Back In The Game!

When trials arrive, it’s a myth you’ll rise to the occasion, rather you’ll sink to your level of preparation. If you’re bringing (metaphorically) a knife to a gun-fight, you’ve set yourself up as a pig going to slaughter. Zechariah and Haggai appear about the same time to encourage the people to rejoin the battle, providing practical advice you can use today. It’s time to transform from a 98-pound spiritual weakling to a seasoned combat-ready Christian, because Christianity is — and always will be — a full-contact experience (whether you know it or not).

The Book Of Daniel

Daniel’s book contains more than mystical prophecy and Sunday School flannel-board stories, as Paul reminds us “whatsoever things were written in earlier times were written for our learning, that we, through patience and comfort of the scriptures, might have hope”. Daniel’s character was unwavering, as he decided early in his life to maintain his devotion to God, no matter the cost. That’s the real lesson to learn, and Daniel’s book provides the blueprint.

The Troubled Church

The Corinthians had their share of problems, among them marriage and divorce, divisions, pride, legalism, spiritual gifts, immorality, and more. Unfortunately the church hasn’t learned from Paul’s letter as the same mistakes repeat over and over … and over. Have you thought about how people outside the church see it today? Their responses reveal much about the state of today’s church — and it’s not good — as the problems at Corinth continue today.
